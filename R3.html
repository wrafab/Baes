Rotation 3 - Roy Bae

Rotation 3: Design / Garment and UV printer



In this rotation, I made a shirt and jigsaw puzzle.



I printed 08:46 on my shirt. Since the day before I made this was 9:11, I wanted to make something represents that tragedy.
8:46 AM is when the first plane hit the world trade center.


Procedure


I first downloaded and installed a font that matched the theme and then typed 08:46 in illustrator, saved as JPG.
After, I opened PDIP, uploaded the JPG file, converted it as ar3 so that I can actually print it on my shirt.
It was printed, then sadly I did not know I was not supposed to touch it, so it got smeared a little bit.
It wasn't a big deal except I could notice that in a close distance.


I also made a jigsaw puzzle. I made a 12" x 12" jigsaw puzzle scheme in Fusion 360 with a bunch of bezier curves.
it was relatively easier than other projects except that my CorelDraw has "unrecoverable error" whenever I try to open the file.


I picked a random meme from the internet. I resized it so that it can fit to 12" X 12" cardboard paper. Roland's UV printer
was not so intuitive compared to other devices we worked with. Plus, someone broke the printer so it took even longer. Since
LEF-20 UV printer is using UV rays, anything that blocks UV cannot be printed on. However, it can print on objects with high
curvature, such as golf balls.
